2. Designing a curation of furtniture

Chairs designed to withstand the test of time, through thick and thin, rough conditions and still be standing on their one or many legs, definately deserves standing ovations. This case study will showcase of I designed an art curation of chairs.

This curation of furtniture is not apart of any of my courses, it is instead just one of the many different design projects I have been working on in the vector graphics editor tool Figma.

I knew I wanted to design a website that exhibits products, but I figured that a full on E-Com website is a larger task than what I had in mind. Oxford Languages defines a curation as "the action or process of selecting, organizing, and looking after the items in a collection or exhibition."

Additionally, the reason why I specifically chose to display furniture is because I was heavily inspired by posts of furniture brands I came across on my instagram, such as Pedrali and Falk. That said, this case study examines how I designed the curation of furniture, "Standing Ovations".

2. Designing the mobile view.
After sending out our surveys we started working on our interviews. I had absolutely no previous experience of conducting interviews, thus different types of interviews and structure methodologies were very new to me. My group and I decided to create an interview template to follow whilst directing our interviews so that our results do not vary too much.

We built a semi-structured interview template which had four different sections; an Introduction, some fixed questions, general part and an outro. This means that some of the acquired information will be quantitative and other qualitative. I also recorded the interview so that I didn't need to transcribe the interview on the spot, and instead be able to do so afterwards.

I made the decision to interview one of my closest friends and retrospectively, this was one of the many mistakes I made. Looking back, listening and transcribing the interview made me realize that if I had the chance to redo it, I would have chosen to interview a different person and ask the questions in a very different manner

For example, I did not really know what I was looking to accomplish in the unstructured section of the interview and I thereby did not conduct that part of the interview in a productive fashion. Shopping behaviors and online shopping regarding food were some of the key subjects that we aimed to gather nuanced information about but I made little to no effort gathering such information, my follow-up questions and ability to come up with questions on-the-spot were simply not good enough.

All in all, these are mistakes I definitely will learn from and they will without a doubt act as important things to consider before I conduct any future qualitative or quantitative methods and activities of information gathering.

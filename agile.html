3. Working with Agile Methods

Agile methods was one of the first courses in my programme at IT Högskolan, and in this course we were given that task of working in sprints. Working in Agile sprints means we follow the principles of satisfying the customer early, through continuous delivery of value.

The course began with a group of classmates and I taking upon the role as newly-acquired UX-Designers for an Energy company in our first assignment. We then were to participate in a seminar to discuss pros and cons regarding the traditional waterfall- and agile development strategies.

Next we began our first sprint, the company my group worked for wanted us to test working with agility in mind. The first sprint consisted of appointing a scrum master, planning in daily stand ups and preparing our work in a Trello Board and bringing fourth an evaluation of a competitors design. The evaluation needed to underline if there is any difficulty finding information, comparing prices or signing agreements.

Our findings then needed to be turned into use cases. Such as “How easy is it to find information about prices as a possible new customer?” and a user story “As a new user I want to be able to see priceinformation so that I can evaluate if its worth to switch energy company”.

Every sprint also ended with a retrospective of what went well, what could have gone better and what can we do better next sprint. And every sprint ended with a meeting with the product owner to access what to be done in the following sprint.

3. What I learned, Mistakes and what would I do differently.
This design project was definately not thorough and thoughtful, instead simply just a design challenge I wanted to try and complete. This was one of the first design projects where I got the slightest aqcuainted with E-com and product interfaces, thus every design choice and decision I made is simply an assumption of what I thinks works best or looks the best.

Since I finished this project, I completed the courses: Business Benefits Of Design, User-Centered Design and Agile Methods at my university. And I now understand that UX-research is such an incredibly vital part of design. If I were to redo the project I would strive towards making informed design desicions, and to achieve this I would work in an iterative design cycle like Lean-UX with 1. Think, 2. Make and 3. Test as the three cycle points to iterate between. The testing section would consist of surveys, usability Testing or other forms of quantitative or qualitative methods of gathering information which I can apply to the design.
